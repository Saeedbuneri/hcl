🤰 What is a Pregnancy Test?

A pregnancy test is a simple test that detects the hCG hormone (Human Chorionic Gonadotropin) in your body. This hormone is only produced during pregnancy. The test tells you whether or not you are pregnant.

⏰ When Should You Take a Pregnancy Test?

  • 7 days after a missed period: This is the best time — accuracy is 99%
  • First morning urine: For urine tests, the first sample of the day is the most accurate (hCG is most concentrated)
  • Blood tests detect earlier: A blood hCG test can detect pregnancy even before a missed period (6-8 days after conception)

❓ When Can a Pregnancy Test Give a False Negative?

  • Testing too early — hCG hasn't built up enough yet
  • Drinking too much water before the test — urine becomes diluted
  • Using an expired test kit

💡 If your urine test is negative but your period still hasn't come, wait 3-5 days and test again — or get a blood hCG test for the most accurate result.
