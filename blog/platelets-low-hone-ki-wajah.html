Causes of Low Platelet Count
Symptoms & Treatment

By Healthcare Clinical Laboratory - Jowar, Buner • Health Guide

🩸 What Are Platelets?

Platelets (thrombocytes) are tiny blood cells that help your body form clots to stop bleeding. When you get a cut or injury, platelets rush to the site and clump together to seal the wound. A normal platelet count ranges from 150,000 to 400,000 per µL.

When platelets drop below 150,000, the condition is called thrombocytopenia. This can be a serious medical concern.

⚠️ Common Causes of Low Platelets

  • Dengue Fever: Platelets drop rapidly during dengue infection — the most common cause in our region
  • Viral Infections: Typhoid, Hepatitis, and other viral infections can suppress platelet production
  • Anemia: Iron deficiency or Vitamin B12 deficiency can also affect platelet levels
  • Bone Marrow Problems: Leukemia and other bone marrow diseases
  • Medications: Certain antibiotics, painkillers, and blood thinners
  • Autoimmune Diseases: ITP (Immune Thrombocytopenic Purpura) — the body destroys its own platelets
  • Pregnancy: Some women develop low platelets during pregnancy
  • Liver Disease: Cirrhosis or chronic liver problems can reduce platelets

🔍 Symptoms — How to Recognize Low Platelets

  • Unexplained bruises (blue or purple marks) without injury
  • Frequent nosebleeds
  • Bleeding gums while brushing
  • Small red dots on the skin (petechiae)
  • Blood in urine or stool
  • Unusually heavy menstrual bleeding

⚠️ If your platelet count drops below 50,000 — see a doctor IMMEDIATELY! Below 20,000 can be life-threatening.

💊 Treatment Options

  • For Dengue: Drink plenty of fluids, rest, and consider papaya leaf extract (consult your doctor)
  • Severe Cases: Platelet transfusion may be required
  • Iron/B12 Deficiency: Supplements and a balanced diet
  • Regular Monitoring: Get CBC tests regularly to track your platelet count
