🩸 What is a CBC Test?

A CBC (Complete Blood Count) is one of the most commonly ordered and essential blood tests. It counts the different types of cells in your blood and helps doctors detect a wide range of diseases — including anemia, infections, and blood disorders.

It is the most frequently performed blood test worldwide and is a standard part of every routine health checkup.

📋 What Does a CBC Test Measure?

  • Red Blood Cells (RBC): These cells carry oxygen throughout your body. Low RBC count may indicate anemia.
  • White Blood Cells (WBC): These fight infections. High WBC count may indicate an active infection or inflammation.
  • Hemoglobin (Hb): The oxygen-carrying protein in red blood cells. Low hemoglobin means anemia.
  • Platelets: Help your blood clot. Low platelets increase the risk of bleeding.
  • Hematocrit (HCT): The percentage of red blood cells in your total blood volume.
  • ESR: Erythrocyte Sedimentation Rate — a general indicator of inflammation or infection.

⚠️ When Should You Get a CBC Test?

  • If you experience weakness, fatigue, or dizziness
  • If you suspect a fever or infection
  • During dengue season — to monitor platelet count
  • As part of a routine health checkup
  • If your doctor recommends pre-operative testing
  • During pregnancy for regular monitoring
